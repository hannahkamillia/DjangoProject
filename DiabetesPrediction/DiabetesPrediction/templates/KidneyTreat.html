Recommendation and Treatment

Overview

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a progressive condition where the kidneys gradually lose their ability to function properly over months or years. The kidneys are vital organs responsible for filtering waste and excess fluids from the blood, regulating electrolytes, and producing hormones that help control blood pressure, make red blood cells, and maintain bone health. In CKD, these functions become impaired, leading to a buildup of toxins in the body and disruptions in overall health. If untreated, CKD can progress to end-stage renal disease (ESRD), where the kidneys can no longer function and require dialysis or a kidney transplant to sustain life.

Stages of CKD

CKD is classified into five stages based on the kidney's ability to filter waste from the blood, measured by the glomerular filtration rate (GFR).

Symptoms

Shortness of Breath

Cardiopulmonary symptoms, including shortness of breath, are also significant.

Nausea and Vomiting

Gastrointestinal issues such as nausea and vomiting are frequently reported.

Pain

About 57% of patients experience pain, which can include muscle cramps and abdominal discomfort.

Fatigue

Fatigue is one of the most prevalent symptoms, affecting over 50% of patients

Prevention & Treatment

DIETARY
  • Low Protein Diets: Low protein diets have been proposed to slow the progression of CKD and delay the need for dialysis. However, evidence on their effectiveness is mixed. Some studies suggest that very low protein diets may reduce the progression to ESKD in patients with advanced CKD (stages 4 and 5), while low protein diets may have little impact on progression or mortality rates.
  • Plant-Based Diets: These diets are rich in fiber, which can improve gut health and reduce the production of uremic toxins. They also have a lower net acid load, which may help mitigate metabolic acidosis, a common issue in CKD patients.
  • Healthy Dietary Patterns: Healthy dietary patterns, such as the Mediterranean diet, DASH diet, and plant-based diets, is associated with a lower risk of CKD progression and all-cause mortality. These diets emphasize the consumption of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and healthy fats, while limiting red meat and processed foods.
MEDICAL TREATMENT
  • Yang/Qi-Invigorating Formulations: These traditional Chinese formulations are believed to improve mitochondrial function, thereby potentially ameliorating CKD.
  • Tetramethylpyrazine (TMP): An active ingredient from the Chinese herb Ligusticum wallichii, TMP has shown potential in preventing acute kidney injury (AKI) and slowing CKD progression through its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.
